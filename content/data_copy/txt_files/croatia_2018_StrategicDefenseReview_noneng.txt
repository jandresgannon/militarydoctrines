# Predgovor

Sigurnost i obrana Republike Hrvatske, njezinih građana, teritorija, materijalnih 

![3_image_0.png](3_image_0.png) dobara te hrvatskog nacionalnog i kulturnog identiteta temeljna je zadaća Vlade kojoj sam na čelu. Obrambeni resor u ispunjavanju te zadaće ima ključnu ulogu. 

Budući da živimo u doba visoke i ubrzane globalizacije koja donosi značajne gospodarske, političke, tehnološke i društvene promjene, sigurnosni izazovi pred kojima stoji obrambeni resor veći su i složeniji no ikada ranije. Stoga naše obrambene sposobnosti moraju biti detaljno preispitane i prilagođene novim i brzo mijenjajućim okolnostima. Razdoblje nakon prethodno provedenoga strateškog pregleda obrane iz 2013. godine obilježeno je narušavanjem europske stabilnosti kako kroz intenziviranje tradicionalnih tako i kroz aktiviranje novih oblika sigurnosnih rizika i prijetnji. 

Time je došlo do zahtjeva za prilagodbom postojećih i razvojem novih obrambenih sposobnosti u svrhu učinkovite obrane naše zemlje i naših građana. U isto vrijeme, povećale su se i usložnile naše međunarodne obveze, ponajprije što se tiče razvoja kolektivnih obrambenih sposobnosti Sjevernoatlantskog saveza te izgradnje Zajedničke sigurnosne i obrambene politike Europske unije. Republika Hrvatska kroz Europsku uniju, NATO, Ujedinjene narode, Organizaciju za europsku sigurnost i suradnju i druge međunarodne organizacije ostvaruje svoje nacionalne interese. Budući da je uspješnost promoviranja i ostvarivanja tih interesa komplementarna i razmjerna snazi i učinkovitosti organizacija čijih smo članica, ispunjavanje novonastalih međunarodnih obveza važna je zadaća. Temeljni poticaj razvoju obrambenog resora u ovome planskom ciklusu dale su odluke Vlade donesene već početkom mandata na 2. sjednici održanoj 3. studenoga 2016. o daljnjem konceptualnom i normativnom uređenju područja sigurnosti, kao općeg okvira za obrambene reforme. Hrvatski sabor je slijedom toga 14. srpnja 2017. donio Strategiju nacionalne sigurnosti, a 27. listopada 2017. Zakon o sustavu domovinske sigurnosti.

Stoga rad na ovom Strateškom pregledu obrane, te nakon njega i na novom Dugoročnom planu razvoja Oružanih snaga Republike Hrvatske, možemo promatrati kao nastavak već započetog procesa redefiniranja obrambenog resora koji se provodi u kontekstu postavljenom novom Strategijom nacionalne sigurnosti.

Uz puno uvažavanje zahtjeva i obveza koje proizlaze iz međunarodnih sigurnosnih i obrambenih aranžmana, kroz čije ispunjavanje se potvrđuje vjerodostojnost Republike Hrvatske kao partnera i saveznika, obrambeni resor se najprije mora razvijati na način da u svakom trenutku bude spreman odgovoriti na zahtjeve za sposobnostima koji proizlaze iz vlastitih nacionalnih potreba. Obrambeni resor mora održavati i razvijati odgovarajuće sposobnosti za odgovor na prijetnje vojnog karaktera, a kao važna sastavnica sustava domovinske sigurnosti, u koordinaciji i sinergiji s drugim nositeljima i sudionicima ovoga sustava, treba unaprijediti, upotpuniti i razviti ključne sposobnosti za potporu civilnom sektoru u odgovoru na druge oblike prijetnji i rizike za nacionalnu sigurnost. # Foreword

Security and defence of the Republic of Croatia, its citizens, territory, material 

![4_image_0.png](4_image_0.png) goods, as well as the Croatian national and cultural identity, are the main tasks of the Government I am heading. The defence sector has a key role in accomplishing these tasks. Since we live in the time of intense and rapid globalization, which is prompting significant economic, political, technological and social changes, the security challenges that the defence sector is facing are now more complex than ever before. Therefore, our defence capabilities should be thoroughly re-examined and adjusted to emerging and rapidly changing circumstances. The period following the implementation of the 2013 Strategic Defence Review has been marked by undermining of European stability caused by escalation of traditional security risks and threats and the emergence of new ones.

Croatia's international obligations have increased and become more complex. This primarily concerns collective defence capabilities of the North Atlantic Treaty and the development of the Common Security and Defence Policy of the European Union. It is necessary, for the purposes of developing efficient defence of our country and its citizens, to adjust our current defence capabilities and develop new ones. The Republic of Croatia is accomplishing its national interests through cooperation with the European Union, NATO, the United Nations and the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe. Strength and efficiency of these organizations is in direct correlation with successful promotion of these interests; thus, fulfilling our newly arisen international obligations is an important task. Initial incentive for development of the defence sector in this planning cycle was brought about by the Government's decisions adopted at the 2nd session held on 3rd November 2016. These guide further conceptual and normative organization of the security area as the general framework for defence reforms. Pursuant to that, the Croatian Parliament adopted the National Security Strategy on 14 July 2017. Furthermore, the Law on the System of Homeland Security was adopted on 27 October 2017. Efforts undertaken in preparation of this Strategic Defence Review, which is to be followed by the development of the new Long-Term Development Plan of the Armed Forces, can be seen as a continuation of the process aiming to redefine the defence sector. This process is being implemented in the context of the National Security Strategy 2017.

Fully acknowledging the requirements and obligations arising from the international security and defence arrangements - the compliance with which confirms the credibility of the Republic of Croatia as a partner and an ally - the defence sector primarily needs to develop in such a way as to be able, at any given moment, to respond to demands for capabilities stemming from own national needs.

The defence sector should sustain existing and develop new adequate capabilities in order to respond to threats of military nature. As an important component of the homeland security system, it should, in cooperation and synergy with other elements and participants in this system, improve and develop key capabilities for supporting civilian sector in response to other forms of risks and threats to national security. I would like to thank all members of the Ministry of Defence and Armed Forces who, with their knowledge, experience and common vision, have contributed to the completion of the document you are presented with. # 1. Introduction

The Defence Law (The Official Gazette No. 73/13, 

![6_image_0.png](6_image_0.png)

75/15, 27/16, 110/17 and 30/18) defines Strategic Defence Review (SDR) as a fundamental document in the field of defence. The SDR represents a final report of the process of reviewing compatibility of strategic concepts and existing and planned defence capabilities with defence requirements deriving from the realities of strategic and security environment.

The Strategic Defence Review is also a source of information on the current state, efforts and intentions in the field of defence for all citizens of the Republic of Croatia, governmental bodies, international institutions, media, economy, academic community and NGOs. As an expression of a wide spectrum of analyses and discussions conducted within defence sector, the Strategic Defence Review outlines recommendations for undertaking measures that will be further developed in other defence planning documents. This document represents a source of guidelines for activities of all components of the defence sector in the upcoming period. It serves as a basis for developing the upcoming Long-Term Development Plan of the Croatian Armed Forces (LTDP).

The main conclusion of the 2013 Strategic Defence Review was that the Republic of Croatia would develop Armed Forces suitable for challenges of the long-term period ahead of us, capable of carrying out its basic constitutional role of defending the country.

At the same time, Armed Forces will possess adequate 

![6_image_1.png](6_image_1.png)

level of readiness, training and equipping so that they are able to share the burden in operations with allies, but also being at disposal to population endangered by natural and technical-technological disasters and other crisis situations. These guidelines are still relevant; however, developments in security environment require review of mechanism of their implementation.

Therefore, it was concluded that social, economic, strategic and security changes that have taken place in the period from the enactment of the previous Strategic Defence Review (2013) and the Long-Term Development Plan (2014) require re-evaluation of the situation. 

Upon implementation of the Strategic Defence Review, the development of a new or revision of the existing Long-Term Development Plan is required.

# 2. Nalazi I Smjernice

## 2.1. Strateško Okružje I Implikacije Za Obranu

Procjena općega sigurnosnog konteksta upućuje na povećanu razinu neizvjesnosti kako u smislu izvora potencijalnih vojnih i nevojnih prijetnji tako i načina na koji bi se 

![9_image_0.png](9_image_0.png)

takve ugroze nacionalne sigurnosti mogle pojaviti. Vanjski i unutarnji aspekti sigurnosti sve su uže vezani i ta bliska veza jedan je od glavnih čimbenika uzetih u obzir pri definiranju prioriteta razvoja Hrvatske vojske, kao i cjelokupnoga obrambenog resora.

Iako se s razumnom razinom vjerojatnosti može pretpostaviti da bliska budućnost ne donosi ozbiljnu konvencionalnu vojnu prijetnju, brzi i nepredvidljivi razvoj geopolitičkih okolnosti ne dopušta nam potpuno je isključiti. 

Suvremene prijetnje nacionalnoj sigurnosti uključuju i neizravno djelovanje, bez nužne namjere ugrožavanja cjelovitosti teritorija Republike Hrvatske, ali s ciljem slabljenja suvereniteta države odnosno utjecaja na državne institucije.

Razvoj novih tehnologija i njihovo korištenje u vojne svrhe, jačanje utjecaja nedržavnih čimbenika u međunarodnim odnosima te tzv. hibridno djelovanje koje uključuje istovremeno konvencionalno, prikriveno, informacijsko i kibernetičko djelovanje, upućuju na to da će buduće vojne aktivnosti biti složenije i višedimenzionalne. 

Postizanje ciljeva u operacijama zahtijevat će stoga snažnu sposobnost prilagodbe promjenama u scenarijima operacija i to u širokom spektru operacija - od onih visokog intenziteta do stabilizacijskih operacija u postkonfliktnom razdoblju i operacija očuvanja mira. 

Izazovi s kojima će se Hrvatska vojska morati suočiti zahtijevaju inovativni i proaktivni pristup tradicionalnim konceptima korištenja snaga. Planiranje i provedba budućih operacija zahtijevat će usmjerenost prema združenim i multinacionalnim snagama, ali i veću interakciju s drugim državnim tijelima kao i institucijama civilnog društva, s obzirom na to da multidimenzionalnim prijetnjama nacionalne granice ne predstavljaju značajniju zapreku. 

U skladu s tim Strategija nacionalne sigurnosti Republike Hrvatske (Narodne novine, br. 73/17), zbog promijenjene i proširene sigurnosne paradigme, poziva na veću sinergiju državnih institucija, privatnog sektora i građana.

Obrambeni resor će stoga trebati razvijati nova znanja, vještine i procedure s posebnim naglaskom na razvoj i održavanje interoperabilnosti sa savezničkim snagama te koordinaciju i suradnju s drugim institucijama društva povezanih u sustav domovinske sigurnosti. 

# 2. **Findings And** Guidelines

## 2.1 Strategic Environment And Implications For Defence

\Assessment of the general security context indicates 

![10_image_0.png](10_image_0.png) an increased level of uncertainty both in terms of the source of potential military and non-military threats and to manners in which such threats to national security could occur. External and internal security aspects have been increasingly interlinked and that close connection is one of the main factors taken into consideration when defining priorities of the development of the Armed Forces and to the defence sector as a whole.

Even though it can be assumed with reasonable level of probability that the foreseeable future will not bring forth a serious conventional military threat, rapid and unpredictable development of the geopolitical circumstances does not allow us to exclude it entirely.

Contemporary threats to national security also include indirect operations, not necessarily with intention of endangering a national territory, but with aim to weaken the sovereignty of the state or to influence the state institutions.

Development of new technologies and their use for military purposes, strengthening of the influence of nonstate actors in international relations and the so-called hybrid activity– which includes simultaneous conventional, hidden, information, and cyber operations - indicate that future military operations will be more complex and multidimensional.

Achieving goals in operations will demand strong capability of adjustment to changes in multiple operational 

![10_image_1.png](10_image_1.png)

scenarios in a wide spectrum of operations - from high intensity operations to stabilization operations in postconflict period and peacekeeping operations. 

Challenges the Armed Forces will have to meet require an innovative and proactive approach to traditional concepts of use of force. Planning and conduct of future operations requires approach based on joint and multinational forces. Furthermore, it demands for a greater interaction with other governmental bodies and civil society institutions, in view of the fact that national borders do not represent a major obstacle to multidimensional threats. 

Accordingly, National Security Strategy, due to changed and expanded security paradigm, calls for greater synergy of the governmental institutions, private sector and citizens.

## Otpornost

U navedenim okolnostima vojnih i nevojnih izazova u području sigurnosti, u kojima je praktično svaki društveni, 

![11_image_0.png](11_image_0.png)

gospodarski ili vojni resurs ranjiv, sve je naglašenija potreba za povećanjem otpornosti države i društva. Otpornost uključuje održavanje i zaštitu ključnih civilnih sposobnosti i njihovu potporu vojnim aktivnostima. 

Republika Hrvatska će stoga razvijati sposobnosti učinkovitog i usklađenog djelovanja civilnih institucija, a tome će se prilagoditi i budući razvoj Hrvatske vojske, kao i drugih institucija u čijim su nadležnostima poslovi važni za obranu i sigurnost. 

Na taj će način Hrvatska vojska moći biti pravodobno i na odgovarajući način upotrijebljena u potpori društvu, ali i obratno, biti potpomognute civilnim resursima - iz sustava energetike, prometa, veza i komunikacije, zdravstva, gospodarstva, diplomacije, civilne zaštite itd.

## Obrambena Suradnja

Međunarodna obrambena suradnja, kao dio vanjske, sigurnosne i obrambene politike Republike Hrvatske, snažan je instrument razvoja obrambenih sposobnosti, jačanja političkih odnosa s partnerima, promicanja nacionalnih interesa u međunarodnim organizacijama, razvoja dobrosusjedskih odnosa i stabilizacije područja od specifičnoga nacionalnog interesa, kao i sveukupnog učvršćivanja međunarodnog položaja Republike Hrvatske. 

Težište međunarodne obrambene suradnje i dalje će biti jačanje suradnje sa strateškim partnerima, djelovanje u okviru Organizacije Sjevernoatlantskoga ugovora (u daljnjem tekstu NATO) i Europske unije, te redefiniranje i razvoj regionalne obrambene suradnje. 

U obrambenoj suradnji sa strateškim saveznicima, uključujući NATO i Europsku uniju, naglasak će biti na razvoju potrebnih obrambenih sposobnosti, jačanju sveukupnih međusobnih odnosa te na postizanju visokog stupnja interoperabilnosti oružanih snaga. Suradnja sa zemljama iz jugoistočne Europe usmjerit će se na potporu njihovim obrambenim reformama, s naglaskom na konkretne aktivnosti koje mogu pospješiti pristupanje NATO-u i Europskoj uniji.

RACVIAC - Centar za sigurnosnu suradnju, kao jedina međunarodna organizacija sa sjedištem u Republici Hrvatskoj, snažan je instrument za jačanje stabilnosti i sigurnosti država jugoistočne Europe te platforma za prenošenje znanja i iskustava u druga područja. Republika Hrvatska nastavit će pružati potporu RACVIAC-u u svim područjima djelovanja. 

Istodobno će se jačati obrambena suradnja sa zemljama srednje Europe u okviru Srednjoeuropske obrambene suradnje, s fokusom na izgradnju obrambenih sposobnosti i koordinaciju djelovanja unutar Europske unije. 

Therefore, defence sector needs to develop new knowledge, skills and procedures with particular emphasis on the development and sustainment of interoperability with allied forces. Likewise, it will need to foster coordination and cooperation with other institutions of the society interlinked within homeland security system. 

## Resilience

In the stated circumstances of military and non-military 

![12_image_0.png](12_image_0.png) challenges in the field of security, in which practically every social, economic or military resource is vulnerable, a requirement for building resilience of the state and the society is increasingly more emphasized.

The resilience also includes protection of the key civilian capabilities and their support to military activities. The Republic of Croatia will therefore develop capabilities of efficient and harmonized operation of different civilian institutions in which sense the future development of the Armed Forces and of other state institutions competent for defence-related affairs will be adjusted accordingly. In this way, it will be possible to employ the Armed Forces as support to society in a timely and appropriate manner. On the other hand, they will be supported by civilian resources - from power supply system, traffic, means of the communication system, health system, economy, diplomacy, civil defence etc.

## Defence Cooperation

International defence cooperation, as part of the foreign, 

![12_image_1.png](12_image_1.png) security and defence policy of the Republic of Croatia, is a strong instrument of the development of defence capabilities, strengthening of political relations with partners, fostering national interests in international organizations, development of good neighbourly relations and stabilization of the area of specific national interest, and overall reinforcement of the international position of the Republic of Croatia.

The main guideline of the international defence cooperation will continue to be on strengthening cooperation with strategic partners, activities within NATO and European Union, redefinition and development of the regional defence cooperation.

With regard to defence cooperation with strategic allies - including NATO and European Union - emphasis will be laid to the development of necessary defence capabilities, strengthening mutual relations and achieving high degree of interoperability of the Armed Forces. # 2.3 Defence Capabilities, Status And Development Perspective

The Armed Forces are the main defence instrument of 

![24_image_0.png](24_image_0.png)

the Republic of Croatia.

In order to timely and adequately respond to threats, challenges and risks in ever-changing security environment, the Armed Forces must be designed as multi-purpose, flexible and modular forces capable for rapid response and integration with other elements of national power.

Considering that conducting the Armed Forces missions implies a broader and international response, they must develop, organize, equip, prepare and train for action, together with the Allies on the territory of the Republic of Croatia and outside the country.

At the same time, the capabilities, the development of which arises primarily from national needs of the Republic of Croatia, must be, in terms of resources and time, optimally synchronized with the capabilities with which the Republic of Croatia contributes within the collective defence system, thus forming a joint, mutually complementing package.

Development of capabilities which have been, as a national responsibility, agreed upon with the allies in the context of NATO Defence Planning Process (NATO Capability Package 2017) will be given priority. On one hand this demonstrates firm commitment to the Alliance capabilities and on the other it will ensure the interoperability with the Allied forces.

The services remain a key element of the capability sustainment, while the use of military capabilities is 

![24_image_1.png](24_image_1.png)

based on jointness through military education, training, exercise and doctrinal solutions. The Armed Forces will continue to join the capabilities of service and functional components with the aim of achieving the synergetic effect in response to strategic challenges. Doctrinal postulates for joint action will be adopted through exercises and experimental methods. Their validity will be tested and the existing solutions improved. The need to redefine territorial deployment of the Armed Forces commands, units and institutions has been reaffirmed in order to ensure military presence according to the specificities of the geographical position and security challenges. Furthermore, this need was addressed to assure a balanced development of national defence capabilities, as well as to facilitate the speed of their employment. Through this process support will be given to quality family life and revitalization of Croatian regions in which the Croatian Army has been traditionally present.

Modernization of the existing capabilities and development of new ones will be approached through the advanced and improved defence planning system which,

## 2.4.3

DEFENCE AS A FACTOR OF ECONOMIC AND SCIENTIFIC-TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENT
Defence industry nowadays, as in the Homeland War, 

![46_image_1.png](46_image_1.png)

has an important role in achievement of necessary military capabilities.

It is one of the factors of the economic development of the country since with its propulsive and high-technology orientation it encourages development of the innovative environment in complementary industrial sectors.

National security interests support the development of defence capabilities with maximal reliance on domestic defence industry and scientific community, with concurrent retention of the highest quality standards of the material assets procured for requirements of the Armed Forces. 

In 2017 a goal was achieved - as set out in the previous Strategic Defence Review - that a Croatian soldier, fully equipped with Croatian weapons and military equipment, would be deployed to international peace support operation in Afghanistan.

Relatively small-scale production, solely for the Armed Forces' requirements, does not provide economically cost-effective investments in development of the complex, technologically advanced systems. Therefore, future of the Croatian defence industry lays in its expansion to international market where it can be recognized only by high-quality of the products. That process will be fully supported by governmental institutions and promoted by our diplomatic network.

Participation in international projects of research, development and production will be particularly encouraged as well as business based on licensing, co-production and sub-contracting for a wider circle of users. 

The importation of ready-made products, technological solutions and systems will be carried out with the greatest possible inclusion of the domestic defence industry and scientific-technological base in their development and production. All this will be undertaken within the framework of mutual interest and cooperation with potential suppliers from abroad.

Oslonac na hrvatsku vojnu industriju bit će kompatibilan s korištenjem svih prednosti koje Republici Hrvatskoj donosi članstvo u Europskoj uniji i NATO-u. U tom smislu intenzivirat će se suradnja s agencijama koje obavljaju poslove nabave i logističke potpore za potrebe NATO-a s obzirom na višestruku korist koju ona donosi Republici Hrvatskoj, kao i korištenje novih EU programa i inicijativa vezanih za razvoj europskog obrambenog tržišta. Na ovaj način osigurava se dostupnost širokog asortimana proizvoda i usluga vrhunske provjerene kvalitete, a s druge strane mogućnost plasmana roba i usluga hrvatskih proizvođača na veliko tržište NATO i EU članica te partnerskih zemalja.

## 2.4.5 Organizational And Managerial Aspects Of The Defence Sector

New National Security Strategy has recognized defence as a component of the national security system and the resources it possesses have considerably contributed to building and implementing this system. The Ministry of Defence and the Armed Forces constitute an important and unavoidable element of this system, with their strength, diversified personnel structure, available equipment, varied capabilities, and ultimately, with great experience gained in Homeland War and in international operations and missions.

The level of inter-sector coordination to date is mainly evaluated as satisfactory. Still, every crisis situation reveals that activities of all institutions involved in solving such situations (forest fires, floods) have not always been synchronized. This is to be resolved through implementation of the newly established homeland security system. Optimal model of inter-sector cooperation will be established and it will provide a synergistic effect of operation of the defence and other sectors in implementation of joint missions. It will avoid overlapping in development of the capabilities and upgrade the mechanisms of gathering and sharing information of shared interest.

Analyses of the past processes warned about drawbacks of the existing information systems and interlinking of the organizational units of the Ministry of Defence and the Armed Forces in the sense of information flow velocity, project management and system management.

Analyses of the business processes will result in recommendations for administrative, organizational and, if necessary, legal modifications of the system to avoid overlapping and to clearly identify competencies and responsibilities.

The legal framework of public finances of the Republic of Croatia, which has been in effect for years, allows for a three-year budget planning perspective.

By economic and fiscal policy guidelines, adopted by the Government of the Republic of Croatia, the amount of the financial plan for the following budget year and projections for the following two years are defined. The Ministry of Defence adjusts its defence planning to this legal framework, although most projects and activities require multiannual financing which surpasses the threeyear period.

Plan za 2017. i 2018. i projekcije za 2019.-2020. financijskog plana Ministarstva obrane Within the National Reform Programme of 2017, submitted to the European Commission, the Government of the Republic of Croatia outlined the establishment of the strategic planning and development management system as one of the key reform measures for strengthening the framework for public finance management and implementation of fiscal consolidation. An Act on the Strategic Planning and Development Management is the central element of this system. Its implementation will create requirements for the adoption of long-term macroeconomic projections which will include multiannual dynamics of the increase in budget defence allocation.

In accordance with NATO budget guidelines, in the coming years the Republic of Croatia will continually increase allocations for defence toward targeted 2% of GDP, taking into account national fiscal and economic abilities. Croatia will also increase the share of defence budget for equipping and modernization toward targeted 20%, by which further development of defence capabilities will be ensured.

Increased defence allocations in 2017 ensured the continuation of strategic projects implementation and created conditions for strengthening of the Armed Forces' defence capabilities. The budget proposal for 2018 and the projections for 2019 and 2020 imply the trend of defence allocation increase. This particularly refers to the defence budget increase in absolute terms, but also to its increase as a share in GDP.

# 3. **Zaključak**

Složenost i nepredvidljivost strateškog okružja, brza tranzicija prijetnji u obliku, opsegu i intenzitetu, pojava novih netradicionalnih prijetnji te tehnološki i društveni trendovi u značajnoj mjeri povećavaju spektar sposobnosti koje Oružane snage trebaju razviti za provedbu misija i zadaća - od povećane vatrene moći i mobilnosti manevarskih snaga sposobnih za sudjelovanje u združenim operacijama visokog intenziteta pa do sposobnosti zajedničkog djelovanja u međuresornom i međunarodnom te tehnološki zahtjevnom okružju.

Republika Hrvatska će nastaviti pridonositi kolektivnoj obrani, kriznom upravljanju i kooperativnoj sigurnosti NATO-a te sudjelovati u jačanju Zajedničke sigurnosne i obrambene politike Europske unije, pridonoseći strateškom partnerstvu i komplementarnosti NATO-a i Europske unije. Uz daljnje jačanje višegodišnje uspješne suradnje sa strateškim partnerima, osnažit će se mehanizmi regionalne obrambene suradnje sa zemljama srednje i jugoistočne Europe. Razvoj sposobnosti temeljit će se na načelima integriranosti i prilagodljivosti Oružanih snaga, uvezujući njihovo djelovanje s drugim sudionicima u sustavu domovinske sigurnosti. U kratkoročnom razdoblju, uz razvoj i održavanje ključnih obrambenih sposobnosti te nabavu višenamjenskoga borbenog aviona, osigurat će se i uvođenje novih i poboljšanje postojećih sposobnosti poput strateškog izviđanja, prikupljanja i obrade informacija, strateškog komuniciranja, kao i sposobnosti iz kibernetičke domene. Pripadnici Hrvatske vojske i državni službenici i namještenici Ministarstva obrane zbog svojih su kompetencija, osobnog integriteta i domoljublja najveća vrijednost i jamstvo provedbe postavljenih zadaća. Stoga će naročita pažnja i napori biti usmjereni na podizanje društvenog ugleda vojnog poziva, brigu o standardima života i rada, profesionalnog razvoja, kao i poboljšanje uvjeta života i rada vojnika, dočasnika i časnika, državnih službenika i namještenika. Republika Hrvatska će slijedeći zaključke NATO Summita u Walesu 
(2014.), nastaviti s kontinuiranim povećanjem izdvajanja za potrebe resora obrane u apsolutnom iznosu kako bi se obrambeni proračun, uvažavajući fiskalne i ekonomske mogućnosti Republike Hrvatske, postupno približio ciljanom iznosu od 2 % BDP-a te da 20 % obrambenog proračuna bude usmjereno na troškove opremanja i modernizacije, čime će se osigurati daljnji rast obrambenih sposobnosti. U Državnom proračunu Republike Hrvatske za 2017. godinu, prvi put nakon višegodišnjeg smanjivanja, povećani su izdaci za obranu, a povećanje se nastavlja i u 2018. godini i projekcijama za 2019. i 2020. godinu. Unatoč tome, promjena strukture troškova ostaje jedan od ključnih izazova u srednjoročnom razdoblju kao preduvjet dostizanja planiranih obrambenih sposobnosti i uravnoteženog razvoja obrambenog resora. 

# 3. Conclusion

Complexity and unpredictability of the strategic environment, rapid transition of threats in form, scope and intensity, emergence of new non-traditional threats and technological and social trends significantly increase the range of capabilities that the Armed Forces need to develop for the conduct of missions and tasks - from enhanced firepower and mobility of manoeuvre forces capable of participating in high intensity joint operations to capability for joint activities in interagency, international and technologically challenging environment.

Defence are the greatest value and guarantee for implementation of the set tasks. Therefore, particular attention 

![64_image_0.png](64_image_0.png)

and efforts will be directed to raising the social reputation of the military profession, care for the quality of life and work, professional development, as well as improvement of living and working conditions. The Republic of Croatia will, following the conclusions of the 2014 NATO Summit in Wales, continue the steady increase of defence budget in absolute terms in order to gradually come closer to the targeted amount of 2% its GDP, taking into account national fiscal and economical capabilities and direct 20% of the defence budget toward equipping and modernization, which will ensure further growth of defence capabilities. The State Budget for 2017, for the first time after the multi-annual decrease, provided an increase of defence expenditures; this trend continues in 2018. Regardless of that, the change of cost structure remains one of the key challenges in the mid-term period as a prerequisite for achieving the planned defence capabilities and harmonized development of the defence sector.

The Republic of Croatia will continue to contribute to the collective defence, crisis management and NATO cooperative security and participate in strengthening the Common Security and Defence Policy of the European Union, contributing to strategic partnership and complementarity of NATO and the European Union. Together with further strengthening of multi-annual cooperation with strategic partners, mechanisms for regional defence cooperation with countries from Central and South-Eastern Europe will also be enhanced. Development of capabilities will be based on the principles of integration and adaptability of the Armed Forces, connecting their activities with other participants within the homeland security system. In midterm period, apart from the development and sustainment of key defence capabilities and acquisition of the new multi-purpose combat aircraft, the introduction of new and improvement of existing capabilities, such as strategic reconnaissance, gathering and processing of information, strategic communication and capabilities from cyber domain will be ensured.
